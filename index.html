Covid-19

Covid 19 is a highly contagious disease that targets the respiratory system. It is first identified in Wuhan, China in December 2019. This virus spreads world wide which leads to it causing a world wide pandemic. The graph below shows the number of cases world wide daily. We can see that number of cases daily keep raising until the end of 2020. It then declined in the early 2021 before reaching it's peak in April.





Vaccines

Because it is a new virus, vaccines are made and are quickly rolled out. The vaccine will boost the antibody of a person and mitigate the spread of Covid 19. This map displays the percentage of fully vaccinated populations in each country. As we can see from the map, the country with high percentage are mostly developed countries.

Deaths

Due to the long period of developing the vaccine, there were times where the spread of Covid 19 cannot be mitigated and many people were sick. This causes a lot of deaths mainly among people with older age. The map visualizes the number of deaths in each country. Note that the more Population the country has, the more deaths there are.

Fortunately, not all covid cases resulted in death. Most people who are infected with the virus managed to recover and heal. The chart below depicts the total cases in every country with different colours that show the total death, total recovered and cases which are still active.
